Lemonade - Beyoncé (2016)

  • Genre: Art-Pop, R&B.
  • Favorite Tracks: "Pray You Catch Me", "6 Inch","Daddy Lessons" "Love Drought" "Sandcastles"

I'm gonna try to talk about this record without getting too personal or opinionated on it. This is a tough one.

"Lemonade" is the very first album that I love, and decided to put here that gives me a migraine. It is an exercise on forgiveness of the human experience. Generational moments from our lifetime that are traitorous or evil... And I don't agree with it.

I spent entire days, HOURS of waking, thinking about the themes, the story, every individual track of this album. Trying to understand, trying to figure out what it means, what it's trying to say while constantly thinking to myself "Am I the only one... Not really agreeing with this?"

In 2015, Jay-Z, who had been married to Knowles for 7 years at that point, was being accused of infidelity. At that point, they already had their first child, so Knowles was understandably frustrated, angry and disappointed. And so, in an attempt to save their relationship, they both decided to go to Paris and work on separate albums discussing this issue. Jay's was the album "4:44" and she made "Lemonade".

This project is considered a "visual" album, with an accompanying movie by the same name. And after having listened to the record by itself and then the movie, I will say that this is an "auteur" project. It's very subtle in some of its themes, but here's a summed up version (At least, from what I got):

This is an album depicting the five stages of grief, through the eyes of someone who is losing her husband, and by consequence, her relationship of 7 years, mirroring what has been happening to her family in the past (her parents) and couples of generations past. In the end, Beyonce decides to break this curse by accepting this mistake, and working together with her partner to build a better relationship with him, and for their daughter. There is also a lot of symbolism referencing black slavery and a celebration of black feminism/ empowerment of women.

As you can tell, this one is not an easy study. Especially since I don't entirely agree with Beyoncé's solution in the end. I won't judge, or belittle. I'll just say that, in the end, Ms. Knowles is a better person than me. Offering next-level empathy and understanding that I'm not sure I could.

Production-wise, this is an extremely varied album. There's ballads on here, mixed with rock, rap and even a bit of reggae. There's also the very first time Beyoncé shows her "country" roots on the song "Daddy Lessons" and let me tell you... She's from Texas, and it shows. It sounds absolutely delightful. Standouts include "Pray You Catch Me" for a really sad, but amazing opener to the record, "6 Inch", for some amazing sample usage and The Weeknd feature, and "Sandcastles", for some of the greatest vocals I've heard coming from Knowles.

And so, a drink for this headache of a record. After some research, I decided I could go with no other drink than a "Lynchburg Lemonade". Symbolic name, symbolic roots, just some Jack Daniel's, triple sec, sour mix and some lemon-lime soda. As Ms. Hattie White once said: "I had my ups and downs, but I always find the inner strength to pull myself up. I was served lemons, but I made lemonade". Make the best of your lives. For yourself and others.
